Husk chips

Nambu Coir Coco Chips are a highly versatile and sustainable product that is made from coconut husks. These chips are supplied in the form of compressed 5kg blocks, which consist of 100% Nambu Coir Coco Peat, ensuring that customers receive a high-quality product that meets their standards. The coco chips are produced by grinding coconut husks into small chips and then drying and compressing them into blocks. This process ensures that the coco chips are free from impurities and contaminants, making them an ideal substrate for horticultural and agricultural applications.

Advantage and Application

Nambu Coir Coco Chips are a high-quality and sustainable substrate that can help improve plant growth and promote sustainable agriculture practices and are ideal for use in a variety of applications, including as a component in potting soil mixes, as a hydroponic growing medium, or as a soil amendment. The compressed blocks are easy to transport and store, making them a convenient option for customers. With on-time delivery and a focus on quality, customers can trust Nambu Coir Coco Chips to meet their needs and exceed their expectations.

125kg Fibre Bale

Nambu Coir Coco Fibres are a natural byproduct of coconut husk processing that has been used for centuiries as a sustainable and eco friendly alternative to synthetic fibres. These fibres are extracted from the outer husk of coconuts and then cleaned, sorted and dried to produce a high quality product.

Advantages and Applications

One of the key benefits of Nambu Coir coco fibres is their high strength and durability. These fibres are naturally resistant to moisture, rot and pests making them an ideal material for a variety of applications. They are commonly used in the manufacture of products such as mats, ropes and brushes due to their strength and resilience. .
